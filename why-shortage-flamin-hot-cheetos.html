Why is there a shortage of Flamin' Hot Cheetos?

 Posted on June 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  622 words  |  Martina Birk

IN THE midst of the coronavirus pandemic, production delays have caused shortages for many consumer goods.

Among these goods is Flamin' Hot Cheetos, which are reportedly becoming harder to find across the nation.

Why is there a shortage of Flamin' Hot Cheetos?

After consumers of Flamin' Hot Cheetos took to their social media accounts to discuss the lack of the snack across the county, the Frito-Lay company announced they are still producing the chip.

"We're still producing this snack," they wrote on Twitter.

"Check out our product locator here, https://fritolay.com/where-to-buy. Thanks!"

Because of "strong consumer demand," Frito-Lay hired 15,000 employees throughout 2021, and stated they plan on adding an additional 5,000 to their roster before the year comes to an end.

"Our strategic investments across key manufacturing sites have significantly expanded our capacity to support strong consumer demand," said said PepsiCo Foods North America chief executive Steven Williams.

PepsiCo owns the Frito-Lay company.

It is understood that several of the company's internal plants are operating at less than full output and more skilled machine operators are needed.

Frito-Lay was also continuing to make investments across its business, including funding new manufacturing lines, warehouse expansions, and distribution improvements, the company said.

Why is there a supply chain crisis?

Because of the coronavirus pandemic, many manufacturers halted production to put safety precautions in place in 2020.

However, with the onset of new variants of the disease and vaccine protocols, things have not yet returned to normal.

The Biden administration attempted to lessen the impact of nationwide demand by requiring one of the largest port systems in America, the Port of Los Angeles, to operate 24/7.

Shortages of packaging materials, seasoning, oil, and starches were also said to be making production challenging, as ocean freight, port congestion, and rail issues are affecting global supply chains, including Frito-Lay.

"We allowed supply chains to get away without having contingencies in place, resiliencies in place, and other measures to ensure humanity would never be subjected to this," said Nick Vyas, the director of the Global Supply Chain Institute at the University of Southern California, according to Vox.

With consumers being urged to shop less, the supply chain may return to normal at some point in the upcoming year.

Another company, Kraft Heinz, has even offered to pay certain customers $20 if they avoid buying cream cheese over the holiday season, as supplies of the popular spread are also at an all-time low.

What are people saying about the decrease in production across the nation?

Many consumers have taken to their social media accounts to address the supply chain issues in America.

One user wrote on their Twitter account: "Please pray for my husband and all supply chain/logistics employees.

"They are on the frontlines of a historic collapse and the government is not helping. Stress is at an all time high along with blood pressure and exhaustion. They are fighting to mitigate the damage.

"It is bad," they added.

The New York Times shared an infographic to their Twitter feed, adding: "Ships stuck at sea, warehouses overflowing, trucks without drivers: The highly intricate and interconnected global supply chain is in upheaval, with little end in sight."

Users have also taken to social media to demand an answer about the apparent Flamin' Hot Cheetos shortage.
