Aaron Boone Father | Career

After attending Stanford University, Bob Boone was drafted by the Philadelphia Phillies in the sixth round (126th overall) of the 1969 amateur draft.

He played as a major leaguer for nineteen seasons, ten of those with the Phillies.

Besides Phillies, he played for California Angels from 1982 to 1988 and Kansas City Royals from 1989 to 1990.

He retired from organized baseball in 1990 and was soon hired by Orlando SunRays as its first manager.

After Orlando, Bob returned to the Royals in 1995 as the team’s manager. However, after two seasons, he was let go.

In 2001, Cincinnati Reds hired Boone to be the skipper replacing Jack McKeon, aka “Trader Jack.”

Throughout nineteen seasons in the MLB, Boone was four times All-Star in the years 1976, 1978, 1978, and 1983.

He made the National League All-Star team three times in a Philadelphia Phillies jersey and helped them win the 1980 World Series.

For his astronomical contribution to the team, Boone was honored with induction into the Philadelphia Baseball Wall of Fame in 2005.

Furthermore, Bob won the Gold Glove Award a staggering seven times throughout his career.
