Stranger Things star reveals season four is 'crazy' and will be 'worth the wait'

 Posted on March 28, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  304 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A MEMBER of the Stranger Things cast has whet fans' appetite by claiming that season four will be 'worth the wait'.

Joe Keery, who plays the role of Steve Harrington in the hit Netflix series, said the delay in production due to coronavirus has been a good thing.

When asked if he could reveal anything about the upcoming fourth season, Joe teased: "It’s crazy! It’s crazy! Hmm, what to say… It’ll be worth the wait, hopefully."

The 28-year-old told The Hollywood Reporter: "It definitely gives them more time to write, and I think that that’s always good.

"I guess there’s the issue of the kids growing, but apart from that, I just think that we want to get the show out as soon as possible for people. That’s definitely true.

"But something that the (Duffer) brothers have always been extremely, extremely controlling over is just the quality control of the show.

"So, if this just gives them more time to realise what their vision is, then yeah, I think it could be a good thing."

Meanwhile, Joe's co-star Natalia Dyer who plays Nancy Wheeler, told The Hollywood Reporter last week that the delay had been a "blessing" for the show's writers.

"Normally, we get the first few scripts, and then they have to keep writing due to the schedule of it, and it takes a while to craft a script," she explained.

"So, normally, there’s a kind of 'writing as we’re going,' but it seems they’ve had enough time.

"I think it’s maybe been a blessing for the writers, in some way, because they had the time to just sit down, think, and create. So, yeah, it appears to be that they’ve got it all down," she added.
