MARSHALLS has announced that it will close all of its stores right before the biggest shopping day of the year.

Black Friday shopping seems to start earlier and earlier every year.

Big stores like Macy's and Walmart have already launched early sales.

However, some companies are still sticking to the typical timeline and waiting to kick off the holiday shopping event.

Marshalls has announced that it will be closed on Thanksgiving, breaking away from other big-name stores that are launching sales on the holiday.

Locations will open early at 7 am on Black Friday so shoppers can get a head start on the holiday savings.

A TJX spokesperson, the company that owns Marshalls, has confirmed to The U.S. Sun that HomeGoods, Sierra, Homesense, and TJ Maxx will also be closed on Thanksgiving.

"We consider ourselves an Associate-friendly company and we are pleased to give Associates the time to enjoy Thanksgiving with family and friends," the spokesperson added.

The department chain already offers heavily discounted products from brands like Nike, Ralph Lauren, Sorel, and more, so Black Friday sales may not offer huge price cuts.

Rather, the store usually offers free shipping or a free gift with purchase.

SHOPPING SPREE

Bargain hunters who are hoping to snag some deals early may be surprised to hear which stores are also closed on Thanksgiving.

For the fourth year in a row, Walmart will close all of its stores on Turkey Day.

“Thanksgiving is such a special day during a very busy season,” said Walmart chief financial officer John Furner in a video clip posted on social media. 

“We want you to spend that day at home with family and loved ones.”

Target is following Walmart's lead and is also keeping its stores closed on holiday.

Its chief executive officer Brian Cornell said in a press release, "Several times, I heard how thankful they [employees] were that our stores would once again be closed on Thanksgiving."

"You don't have to wonder whether this is the last Thanksgiving you'll spend with family and friends for a while because Thanksgiving store hours are one thing we won't 'get back to' when the pandemic finally subsides."
