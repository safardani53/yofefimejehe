Tim McCarver: Career

Tim’s professional career began at just 17 when the St. Louis Cardinals took him under their wings to develop him.

Subsequently, he stayed with the franchise for a decade. And during that decade, McCarver had the best moments of his career.

To explain, Tim won two World Series in 1964 and 1967, playing as the starting catcher in both those series. Furthermore, Tim also became a two-time All-Star in 1966 and 1967.

However, those were the best days of McCarver’s because, for the next decade, the Tennesse native switched between various teams like Philadelphia Phillies, Boston Red Sox, and Montreal Expos.

Tim returned to his former franchise, St. Loius Blues, during the 1973-74 season.

But, sadly, he could never replicate what he achieved during his time with the Blues. As a result, Tim retired from the game in 1979 to focus on his broadcasting career.

Upon his retirement, McCarver became the 29th player in MLB’s history to play in four decades.

What was Tim McCarver’s MLB statistic like?

Is Tim McCarver in the Hall of Fame?

Tim McCarver isn’t in the National Hall of Fame but in the Cardinals Hall of Fame. In the Cardinals Hall of Fame, Tim is the only catcher in franchise history to play in two World Series-clinching games.
