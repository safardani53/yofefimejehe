Nail rhinestones are delicate ornaments for your nails that are quick to jazz up any boring nail art design in a jiffy. These decorative little stones are widely used by nail artists around the globe to create mainly formal and glamorous nail art designs for festive and celebratory occasions. Whether it’s on an accent nail or an all-over design, a set of matching rhinestones are a little-effort way to achieve party nails in no time. Although nail rhinestones come in a variety of different shapes and sizes, along with a wide range of colors and materials. Therefore, it’s crucial to know which collection is the right one for you and your desired nail art designs.

What Are Nail Rhinestones Made Of?

A common question that customers always ask is regarding the material of the rhinestones. Most professional nail rhinestones are made from glass. Manufacturers use a particular kind of glass called transparent k9 glass to create these rhinestones. Nail rhinestones made from glass are normally more expensive and luxurious than ones made from plastic.

How Are Nail Rhinestones Made?

Glass manufacturing

While there are various methods of cutting and creating nail rhinestones, one particular process of making these rhinestones includes the use of a unique diamond cutting machine that precisely shapes these rhinestones into individual shapes. This process is typically more costly and labor-intensive than molded rhinestones, as the rhinestones require several cutting sessions, along with individual polishing at the end.

Molding

Another widely used method for creating these shiny and delicate stones is through a molding process. In this process, the rhinestones are typically all made in one go, which is why this method is the cheapest of all. To start with, the raw material is injected into the mold, before which the mixture is left to harden.

How to Apply Nail Rhinestones to Nails

While it may be daunting at first, applying rhinestones to nails is a relatively uncomplicated process that you can get better at with time. Although perfecting this craft may take some time, starting out with some simple and easy designs is a piece of cake for most users. There are a few critical steps you ought to follow in order to ensure a clean and mess-free application that will last you a good while.

Application:

  • Start by applying a rhinestone adhesive to the part of your nail where you want the rhinestone to be. There is no need to apply the adhesive to the entire nail unless your desired design calls for it.  It's also useful to apply the adhesive to one nail at a time to ensure you're happy with the results before curing.
  • Next, it’s time to place the rhinestones onto your nails. It’s always handy to employ a rhinestone applicator in this regard. Use the applicator's waxy side to carefully lift the rhinestone and position it onto your nail. You may move the rhinestone around with the applicator to settle it into the correct place.
  • After you’re happy with how all your rhinestones look, then it’s time for curing. To cure your newly created design, place your hand under an LED lamp. In case you don't have access to a LED lamp, it’s also possible to make use of a nail polish top-coat instead of an adhesive. However, this method does decrease your design longevity and the stones have a higher risk of falling off.
  • The fourth and final step of rhinestone application is to apply a topcoat. Finish off your design by applying a generous amount of top-coat nail polish to your nails once they’re done curing. It’s important to keep in mind to never apply the top coat on top of the stones and to only use it around the stones. Coating your rhinestones with a top coat can drastically reduce their sparkle and shine.

Tips And tricks:

There are some useful pointers to keep in mind when applying rhinestones, in order to achieve a clean and long-lasting design.

  • It’s crucial to ensure that your nail polish is completely dry before applying the adhesive to your nails. In the case of dip powder nails or acrylics, it’s generally recommended to apply the rhinestone to your nails before the top-coat application.
  • It’s useful to make sure the rhinestones are placed in such a place that they won’t easily get caught up in things and pop off. Therefore, considering the design and amount of rhinestones beforehand can be useful in achieving a long-lasting design.
  • In case you don’t have a rhinestone applicator, you can simply make use of a wax pencil, or a pair of tweezers to carefully pick up and place the rhinestones on your nails.
  • To make sure your rhinestones last long and don’t pop off after a couple of days, it’s wise to place them near your cuticle area on your finger. This is because the likelihood of your stones coming off is greater when they’re placed on the tip of your nails as compared to the base.

How to Care for Nail Rhinestones

After all the effort that goes into carefully placing and curing a rhinestone nail art design, it’s important to take good care of your nails to prevent them from falling off prematurely and ruining your design. Here are a few quick tips that can help you care for your rhinestone nails.

  • When washing dishes, it’s useful to wear gloves to ward off any additional damage that the water may cause to your nails.
  • Reduce the amount of time you spend in the water, whether it be swimming or taking long baths.
  • Use a strong, high-quality adhesive to make sure your design lasts as long as possible.
