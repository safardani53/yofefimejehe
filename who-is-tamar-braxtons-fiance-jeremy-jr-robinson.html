AFTER experiencing a challenging upbringing, the lawyer built a successful career and has now found love.

Singer Tamar Braxton and Jeremy "JR" Robinson got engaged on Peacock's dating show, Queens Court.

Who is Tamar Braxton's fiancé Jeremy 'JR' Robinson?

Robinson is a criminal defense and personal injury attorney based out of Atlanta, Georgia who founded the JR Law Group.

He was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, and had a difficult upbringing.

In August 2021, he opened up about the hardships he has faced to Shoutout Atlanta, revealing that he was raised by a single mother who struggled with drug addiction.

After making some poor life choices, Robinson joined the military.

He said: "By age 16, I had two gold teeth, two felonies and ultimately obtained my GED in juvenile jail.

"I joined the Marine Corps and turned my life around. I served in combat in Iraq and was awarded an Airstrike Flight medal after surviving a rocket propelled grenade attack."

Robinson then spoke with the publication about his successful career while discussing the values of his business.

He said, in part: "At The JR Law Group, we pride ourselves in making sure that we handle our clients with the dedication needed to provide individual consultations on a personal level. We focus on relationships and not volume.

Does Jeremy 'JR' Robinson have kids?

As reported by Hollywood Life, Robinson is divorced and has five children with four different women.

The outlet noted that in the Queen's Court finale, the lawyer discussed having fathered two kids with two different women in 2019.

“That was a very difficult time. It was a dark time. While I was usually dating, I dated multiple women. And I ended up with two beautiful children. I’ve been as transparent as I can be."

The father-of-five added that he is "decent and cordial" with the mothers of his children.

When did Jeremy 'JR' Robinson propose to Tamar Braxton?

Robinson proposed to Braxton during the season one finale of Queens Court, which premiered on Peacock on Thursday, March 16, 2023.

As PEOPLE reported, Robinson said: "Tamar, I'm here forever because I don't see my life without you," the attorney said. "I love you, Tamar. Tamar, will you marry me?"

Braxton became emotional and shook her head "yes."

The publication also discussed how the couple formed a bond over the losses they have faced in their lives.

In March 2022, Braxton's sister Traci Braxton died from cancer. Robinson's mother also passed away from the disease.

People noted: "Robinson said he saw a hummingbird at his mother's funeral, and according to Braxton, her sister said she would become a hummingbird when she died."

The television show's finale included an update that revealed the pair are currently planning their nuptials six months after their engagement.

In October 2023, Robinson confirmed on Instagram that the couple had gone their separate ways.

He said, "Yes, Tamar and I are no longer together. I ended the relationship to focus on getting back to positive energy and being a better person."

Months later, the couple shared that they were engaged again on Christmas.

Tamar shared the re-engagement video on Instagram.

The caption said, "3 months ago we thought we HATED each other and we were completely OVER"

"Here we are after a simple football game box you got for your birthday and one REAL HARD conversation," the post continued.
