I'm a lottery expert - the worst odds for winning revealed and which scratch-offs to stay away from

 Posted on September 12, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  466 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A LOTTERY expert has revealed for players everywhere the worst odds for different games.

In a popular TikTok video, the lotto pro also explained which scratch-offs strategic gamblers should avoid.

Joey Adelman, known as @nibulousnebula on TikTok, gave readers little-known information about instant lottery games.

In his insider video, he pulls out a scratch-off labeled "$50, $100, or $500, which he claims to have the worst odds.

"These tickets right here, or anything named like this (with cash prize numbers) is the worst odds of winning," with just a one in eight chance."

"That's the worst of any ticket, no other ticket is even close to that."

A regular player of scratch lotto and creator of lottery content, Joey explains, that most other tickets have a one in four chance of earning the player a prize.

"The best chances of winning are if you spend the most money," Joey revealed about the "top row" scratch-offs.

High-price scratchers that cost players "$20 or $30" are alleged by the expert to have a one in three chance of being a winning ticket.

Even the dollar tickets, Joey claimed, boast one in four odds.

Still, Joey uses his final moments of the video to again steer users away from the $10 scratcher with the one in eight odds.

"I'm not saying you won't win, I'm just saying its the least likely."

Professor Essa, a math teacher who creates "a range of statistics and calculus videos" on YouTube, also shared some numbers secrets on winning scratchers.

Essa says she uses theories "based on science, probability and math" to share tricks on how to win off scratch tickets.

One of her first essential tips is to make sure the card you purchase actually has prizes remaining to win.

Essa says: "At any time, ten percent of cards have no prizes left at all. You don't want to pay those games."

Once you've had a look at the lottery website and found the cards you shouldn't be playing, then you can purchase ones you feel confident you might have a shot at.

One of the easiest ways to up your chances is to buy in bulk, according to Essa.

She says: "This might cost you several hundred dollars or more, but there's some very good science behind this tip.

"Scratch-off tickets prizes are not random, the winnings tend to be evenly spaced throughout the rolls.
