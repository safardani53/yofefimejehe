 Posted on February 28, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  891 words  |  Aldo Pusey

GRAND NATIONAL winner Lucinda Russell has opened up about the "excruciating pain" she felt after the sad death of a stable favourite.

Mighty Thunder, who won the Scottish Grand National in 2021, died after suffering an aneurysm in the stables at Ayr races on Saturday.

The horse had unseated jockey Patrick Wadge in this year's race but fell ill once he had finished the race.

Russell confirmed the tragic news which occurred just a week after the stable had landed and emotional second Grand National victory with Corach Rambler.

With the sport under attack from animal rights activists, Russell wrote the emotional Facebook post to remove any speculation surrounding the horse.

She said: “We were all devastated to lose Mighty Thunder.

“Having unseated Patrick in the Scottish National, Mighty was caught and returned to the stables looking fine.

"Unfortunately, he then suffered from an aneurysm, a hole in an artery, and died in the stable area.

“Both Hannah, his devoted groom, and Nicola and Colin, his owners, were with him and the whole event was handled with care and compassion by the vets."

She continued: “I have been dreading to write this; the yard and those close to him were in shock and I do not know who posted the news on social media.

“Losing any horse is hard but it is excruciatingly painful to lose one who was loved from the minute that he walked into the yard seven years ago and who took us all on such a journey including the Scottish Grand National and Edinburgh National in 2021.

“I guess that the current mood of social media makes us uncomfortable to share news of our loss but that is trivial compared to the feeling of emptiness and sorrow that Hannah, Colin and Nicola had as they woke this morning.

“Mighty lived the life of an athlete; he was fed well, exercised carefully and allowed to be a horse in the field and in the stable. He knew he was adored and goes down in history with his brave wins.

“His death was due to a weak blood vessel wall; common in pleasure horses, racehorses, humans and other mammals.

"If he had not run yesterday it would have been an issue when he next galloped, in the field or at home. It is tragic to lose him.

“I know that many of you will want to comment on this post, but please rather than that spend a few moments thanking the horses that you know and Mighty for his bravery and kindness.

“He filled our world with love and we will never forget him. A heart will tell us that you care and understand as we are all overwhelmed at the moment.”
