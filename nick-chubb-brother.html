Family Background Of Henry II Chubb

Henry Edward Chubb II is famous as the elder half-brother of NFL player Nick Chubb. Though he also has an athletic career- he played college football at Troy University & GSU before switching to a career as a coach.

Henry II Chubb was born on March 22, 1987, in Georiga to Henry Chubb Sr. and his previous wife, Sheena.

His father later married Lavelle Chubb (only to divorce in 1999, when his half-brother Nick was four years old).

He has three younger half-siblings from his father’s side: Zach Chubb, Neidra Chubb, and Nick Chubb, an NFL player.

Moreover, from his mother, Sheena’s other marriage to Anthony Causer, he has two other half-brothers: DeMarius Causer and Demetrius Causer.

Henry’s father, Henry Sr., is a former U. S. Air Force who served the country in Desert Storm but retired after an eye problem during the war in the Middle East.

Moreover, his father was an athlete, too- he played as a fullback at Valdosta State in the 1982 & 1983 college football seasons.

Nick Chubb’s Brother Henry Chubb | Education & Collegiate Career

Henry II Chubb attended Ware County High School from 2001 to 2005 & was considered a two-way star.

As a senior, he led the team to a 12-2 record and completed 105-of-165 pass attempts for 1,340 yards and 12 touchdowns. He was named All-State by the Atlanta Journal-Constitution.

Then, on June 30, 2005, Henry enrolled at Troy University and played for two seasons: 10 games in 2005 (10 tackles) and 13 games in 2006 (made 35 tackles).

After his sophomore year, he transferred to Georgia Southern University, & was chosen for North-South All-Star Game in Georgia. But his college football was cut short due to a career-ending injury.

In May 2010, he graduated with a Sports and Fitness Management major.

Are Nick Chubb & Bradley Chubb Brothers?

Well, Nick and Bradley are cousins, and both of them are NFL players- two of the best players in the NFL in their respective positions.

Nick Chubb is a running back for the Browns, while Bradley plays for Miami Dolphins (2022- now) after playing for Denver Broncos (2018-22).

Additionally, they are related and are a part of a very influential southern family. His father, Henry, and Bradley’s father, Aaron, are biological brothers.

Chubb Family History | Discovery Of Chubbtown

Georgia, the oldest state in the nation, is home to several vital tales & significant incidents that have shaped American history. The innumerable tales of courageous and tenacious Georgians are carved in history.

And the Chubb family is among them, who are the founders of Chubbtown, Georgia.

Circa 1851, eight Chubb brothers (ancestors of NFL player Nick & his family) went to Georgia. They were formerly enslaved people who had been emancipated in North Carolina.

The Chubb brothers first established themselves in Madison, Morgan County, before making their way west to a piece of land near Cave Spring & Rome in Floyd County. They finally gathered together to form “Chubbtown,” an unorganized settlement.

But anything said is easier than done.

The continuous efforts to settle a residential area became fruitful gradually. They brought valuable talents to any frontier community as farmers, millers, distillers, and blacksmiths.

Not only the Chubbtown thrived & continued to survive despite the Jim Crow law implementation, but they also managed to escape the damage of the Civil War when Sherman sliced his way south to Atlanta.

By the 1870s, the Chubbtown residents had enough land to maintain pastures, fields, a church that is now listed on the National Register of Historic Places, simple dwellings, and structures, some of which are still in existence.
