Gretchen Wilson Career 

Gretchen Wilson is a well-known singing sensation in the music industry.

Wilson moved to Nashville to pursue a musical career and was eventually discovered by Big and Rich. She went on to have two platinum-selling albums in 2003 and 2005.

In 1996, after performing for several months as lead singer for a local cover band, Wilson moved to Nashville to pursue a career in music as she was not getting much fame in her place.

But it was very exhausting and tough for her. It was not easy for her to become successful in one night.

For four years, she worked in bars waiting for her big break. It came in the form of singer-songwriters Big & Rich, who heard her sing one night and, impressed by her talent, introduced her to the Nashville music community.

Gretchen Wilson eventually signed with Sony Music Nashville in 2003. Her debut album, Here for the Party, was certified quadruple platinum thanks in no small part to the chart-topping single “Redneck Woman.”

Her 2005 follow-up, All Jacked Up, debuted at No. 1 and was also certified platinum. This came as a lucky break for her.

Gretchen Wilson has continued to nurture her career by performing with such country music legends as Brooks & Dunn and Montgomery Gentry.

In 2004, she won a Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance and CMA’s coveted Horizon Award.

A year later, CMA named her Female Vocalist of the Year. She has also given many other hit albums, some of which are mentioned below.

Her other famous albums include: One of the Boys (2007), I Got Your Country Right Here (2008), Right on Time (2013) Under the Covers (2013), and Christmas in My Heart (2013).
