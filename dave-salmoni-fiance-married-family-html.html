Canadian native Dave Salmoni has an extreme passion for the wild, and he is a famous animal trainer and television personality who gained massive popularity after hosting wildlife series like Living with Tigers, Animal Face-Off, and Rogue Nature. He is also a known producer of The Trapper and the Amazon(2010), After the Attacks(2008) and Pet Nation Renovation(2016). He was voted as  Sexy Man of the Week by People magazines on 20 November 2009.

Sarnia native studied zoology at the Laurentian University in Sudbury, Ontario. Also, he is certified in Chemical Immobilization of Wildlife. Unable to satisfy his curiosity he wrote his undergraduate thesis on tracking the herniation of Canadian black bears. To master his passion, he later began an apprenticeship with Bowmanville Zoological Park. He got an opportunity to crafts his skill with a legendary animal trainer, Michael Hackenberger.

Dave Salmoni Could Have Died In 1999

Salmoni has always been fascinated by wild animals. His unrestrained imagination has sometimes brought him troubles.

Once, in August 1999 in Ontario, he got attacked by one of his trained big cats named Bongo, a five hundred pound male African Lion.

After such life-threatening incident, an average person would have quit their profession, but not Dave. His love for wild animals rather inspired him to make After the Attack TV show to tell stories of heroism and bravery of animal-attack survivors like him.
