Jack Willis suffers sickening knee injury for England as ITV refuse to show replays before he's stre

 Posted on June 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  535 words  |  Larita Shotwell

JACK WILLIS suffered an horrific knee injury just five minutes after coming on and scoring for England during their win over Italy.

The Wasps flanker, 24, came off the bench to score a try almost immediately.

But his dream of a start turned into a nightmare when he suffered an injury so graphic that broadcasters ITV refused to show the replay.

Willis was forced to battle back from knee and ankle surgery in 2018 after first being named in Eddie Jones' squad.

And after scoring his second try in just his third Test, Willis was rolled at a ruck as his left knee imploded.

The screams of pain echoed around an empty Twickenham with England leading 34-11 against the Six Nations whipping boys.

With Jones having already thrown on his back-five replacements, winger Anthony Watson had to pack down at No8 in the scrum.

England would go on to win 41-18 as both teams traded converted tries late on as the hosts picked up an all-important bonus point.

But Willis is almost certain to miss the rest of this Six Nations.

Jones will now be forced to make do without him for crunch games against Wales, France and Ireland.

After the game, the Red Rose boss said: "We are all concerned, he is on crutches and will be examined on Sunday.

"It didn't look good, and it is probably not good, but we will wait and see.

"It's some sort of knee injury, but he's back in the dressing room with us."

England legend Matt Dawson predicted Willis is set for a 'long lay-off' after the horror injury.

Dawson told BBC Radio 5 Live: "On the clear-out Jack Willis has got his leg jammed.

"No-one likes to see that, especially someone like Dan Lydiate who has been fighting back after a long injury.

"Straight away you thought he was in trouble. His left knee - it buckled underneath him.

"That, unfortunately, is going to be a long lay-off for Jack Willis."

The incident left fans a mixture of devastated and furious - with many calling for players rolling out of rucks to be banned.
