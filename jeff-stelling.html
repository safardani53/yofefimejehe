Career

Jeff Stelling started his profession immediately after graduating from high school. He worked at Hartlepool Mail for four years after finishing school. This was the beginning of his career. Then he became a Middlesbrough F.C. reporter for Radio Tees. That was in the 1970s. He used to work as a sports broadcaster for LBC radio’s Sportswatch show in the 1980s. He then worked for the BBC. In the 1980s, he covered four Olympic Games in Los Angeles and Seoul for BBC 2. He has worked for a number of networks, including Eurosport, TV-am, Channel 4, and others. Finally, he joined Sky Sports in 1992. He was hired as a sports presenter for the program Sports Saturday after working for a few years.

That program is currently known as Gillette Soccer Saturday. He has been working with Sky Sports since then. He also presented Countdown on Channel 4 beginning in 2008. Similarly, he presented the Champions League broadcast from 2011 through 2015. In 2016, he also hosted the ITV game show Alphabetical. For his hard work, the Sports Journalists’ Association named him Sports Broadcaster of the Year for many years in a row. He has also served as the president of Hartlepool United Football Club since 2015.
