Biz Markie dead: Just a Friend rapper passes at 57 after 'battle with diabetes as wife held his hand

 Posted on March 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  877 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

RAPPER Biz Markie died on Friday aged 57.

The Just a Friend hitmaker passed away after his “battle with diabetes as his wife held his hand during his last breath,” according to reports.

TMZ confirmed the rapper’s passing, as a family source said he passed away in a Baltimore hospital at 6:25 on Friday night.

His wife, Tara Hall, reportedly held his hand as he took his last breath.

The nursing staff was on hand to support Biz’s family, who assured his loved ones that he was strong while fighting his health battles.

A rep for Biz confirming his passing to PEOPLE, stating: "It is with profound sadness that we announce, this evening, with his wife Tara by his side, Hip Hop pioneer Biz Markie peacefully passed away.

"We are grateful for the many calls and prayers of support that we have received during this difficult time.

"Biz created a legacy of artistry that will forever be celebrated by his industry peers and his beloved fans whose lives he was able to touch through music, spanning over 35 years," it continued.

"He leaves behind a wife, many family members, and close friends who will miss his vibrant personality, constant jokes, and frequent banter.

"We respectfully request privacy for his family as they mourn their loved one."

LONG HISTORY OF HEALTH PROBLEMS

Biz’s passing comes after it was revealed "he was in a hospice with significant brain damage" following a stroke earlier this month.

It was previously believed he had passed away, with fans of the rapper describing his situation as "brutal" as the false death rumors were spread online.

His rep told Rolling Stone at the time: “The news of Biz Markie’s passing is not true.

“Biz is still under medical care, surrounded by professionals who are working hard to provide the best healthcare possible.”

Reporter Brandon "Scoop B" Robinson also tweeted to quash rumors Biz had died - but added he has "significant brain damage."

Roland Martin gave an update on the singer's condition on Twitter: "Folks, @BizMarkie has NOT died. I have been in contact with sources who are texting and talking to his wife.

"My source talked to Biz TODAY. Per his wife, Biz HAS NOT passed away. Please stop responding to non-credible sources. This hurts the family, friends and fans."

A 'FUN' FATHER

While Biz was in hospice in June, Tara took to Instagram to share a series of videos and photos of the rapper spending time with his children.

She wrote: "You can only imagine what kind of Dad Biz is… fun, fun, fun! Happy Father’s Day @officialbizmarkie ..and to all the Loving Fathers out there…keep making memories.

"They mean so much to us right now…until we can make more," she added.

"And as always, thank you for your kind messages, phone calls, video messages and prayers. Biz loves them and is still in it to win it"

The Clown Prince of Hip-Hop - who was born Marcel Hall - suffered from health issues in the past.

Back in April, Big Daddy Kane revealed Biz was recovering from a stroke.

“He’s getting better,” Kane said. “He’s in rehabilitation now. He’s getting better and stronger every day.”

Last July, he was hospitalized for several weeks due to complications from Type 2 Diabetes, TMZ reported.

REMEMBERED AS A HITMAKER

Biz was best known for his hit song Just a Friend, which was released in September 1989.

As the lead single from his album Biz Never Sleeps, Just a Friend became the rapper’s biggest hit and reached number nine on the Billboard Hot 100 chart in 1990.

Biz's brother and fellow rapper Diamond Shell paid tribute to the Just a Friend singer on Facebook, writing: "To the baddest in the beats icon legend... my brother BIZ MARKIE... I'll miss you more than words."

LL Cool J also took to Instagram to react to the news.

After wiping away his tears, he said: "I just want to say rest in power to Biz Markie, my friend. We have so many memories, man."

After sharing Biz was in LL Cool J's grandmother's basement when he wrote Rock the Bells, he added: "We used to always hang out back in the days. Running around Queens, Long Island. And I'm glad we got to do what we got to do towards the end."

He concluded: "Love you, bro. And I want to send love to all your fans, millions of fans around the world. Your family. Send my love to your family, to your wife."
