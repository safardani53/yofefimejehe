Inside The View host Whoopi Goldbergs wild birthday bash featuring drag queens, massive giveaways

 Posted on July 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  440 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

THE View's Whoopi Goldberg will soon turn 67 years old, and her co-hosts have held a wild birthday bash for her, which featured drag queens and giveaways.

Whoopi and the rest of her panel cut loose for the special day.

When the episode aired on November 10th, Whoopi explained why she was celebrating ahead of her actual birth date on November 13th.

"We're celebrating today because we usually celebrate together," Whoopi told the audience.

"Sunday is the actual day. We're not working, so we're doing it today."

To mark the occasion, guest Emily Blunt handed over a bouquet to The View moderator.

As the festivities continued, Whoopi spoke about her "favorite things" to co-host Sara Haines, 45.

The actress showed off the beauty products from Itinera, her obsession with sneakers, and the Funko Pop collectibles. 

Whoopi even offered a pair of Kizik sneakers to each audience member. 

Before the end of the program, a lineup of drag queens gathered around the birthday girl.

Whoopi said to the audience: "I'm exhausted. I could sure use a break, like a vacation. 

"So could you! Since vacations are also my favorite thing, you're all going on a trip to Cancun, Mexico!"

After the audience stood up and cheered, Whoopi danced along with the drag queens from her seat and shouted: "This has been a great birthday! 

"Happy Birthday to the world!"

NEW CAREER CHANGES

Also during the broadcast, the ABC star announced her new career move and showed off her drink, Whoopi Prosecco.

A group of dancers came out onto the set and brought a bottle of Whoopi Prosecco for each member of the panel.

Whoopi held her bottle up to the camera and said: "So how about some bubbly from my brand new line of Whoopi Prosecco?

"I love hosting gatherings with friends and family - that's why I created Whoopi Prosecco."

The comedian claimed the $21.99 drink is made from "organic grapes" from Asolo, Italy.

ON THE BIG SCREEN

The star also announced she landed a role in an upcoming Western movie written and directed by Mario Van Peebles.

Deadline reported that filming recently wrapped up in Montana.

According to the outlet, the movie centers on: “Chief (Van Peebles), a renegade cowboy putting together a multicultural team of new and old friends to ride into the dangerous unsettled territory to retrieve gold from an abandoned mine.”

The "true-to-life" role of Stagecoach Mary will be played by Whoopi, who has joined an ensemble cast that includes Cedric the Entertainer, John Carroll Lynch, Edward James Olmos, and M. Emmet Walsh.
