FUBAR A Comedy Series Is Set To Premiere On Netflix

 Posted on September 26, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  496 words  |  Martina Birk

FUBAR is a new espionage adventure comedy series starring Arnold Schwarzenegger that will premiere exclusively on Netflix on Thursday, May 25, 2023, at 3:00 a.m. Eastern Time (ET). The next series was created by Nick Santora, best known for The Sopranos, Law & Order, and Scorpion. FUBAR will tell the riveting tale of a father-daughter combination whose lives are flipped upside down once they find they are both working covertly for the CIA. The series will follow their adventurous experiences when a high-risk covert assignment goes wrong.

Since Netflix unveiled the official trailer for the forthcoming new espionage series, it has received a lot of good attention from the public owing to its intriguing and exciting narrative and promising main actors. Viewers have been anticipating what exploits FUBAR has in store for them. Learn all there is to know about FUBAR before it premieres on Netflix.

What can we anticipate from Netflix’s new espionage adventure series?

As previously stated, FUBAR will be launched on Netflix on Thursday, May 25, 2023, at 3:00 a.m. ET. The impending new series will consist of eight episodes, all of which will be published on the same day. Adam Higgs, Scott Sullivan, Penny Cox, Lillian Wang, Cait Duffy, Michael J. Gutierrez, and creator Nick Santora are among the series’ many writers.

Netflix’s official description for the series goes as follows:

“When a father and daughter discover they’ve both been secretly working as CIA agents for years, they realize their entire relationship has been a lie and they truly don’t know each other at all.” FUBAR, forced to work together as partners, confronts universal family issues against a worldwide background of espionage, amazing action, and comedy.”

Take a closer look at the series’ official trailer here:

The official description and teaser provide fans with hints and insights into what they may anticipate from the upcoming series. By all appearances, the audience is in for a thrilling roller coaster journey as the father-daughter team embarks on a fascinating sequence of adventures as undercover CIA operatives. Lead actor Arnold Schwarzenegger told Netflix’s Tudum about the series:

“Everywhere I go, people ask when I’m going to do another big action comedy like True Lies,…Well, here it is.”

Santora, the series creator, and showrunner, has also spoken to Tudum about the project, noting:

“By far the most bizarre undertaking of my career,…I grew up watching Arnold Schwarzenegger movies — I’d beg my father for a few dollars so I could go to the cinema and see the greatest actor in the world on the big screen — so making Arnold’s first written television project is really thrilling for me.”

He went on to say:

“I’ve always admired Arnold Schwarzenegger’s ability to be funny while still kicking ass… that’s why I wanted FUBAR to be a hysterical, CIA-spy comedy mixed with heart-stopping action!” And it is all of these things — and more. I’d want to go into further detail, but it’s classified.”
