Abba set for 1billion Sin City deal as Las Vegas hotels start bidding war for live show

 Posted on March 17, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1423 words  |  Aldo Pusey

THEIR comeback after almost 40 years saw them land a No1 album and their first Grammy nod, for new song I Still Have Faith In You.

But now ABBA could be on course to make some serious Money, Money, Money — after three Las Vegas hotels started a bidding war for the Swedish supergroup’s “Abbatar” concert show Abba Voyage.

Insiders revealed how it was hoped the ground-breaking show, which kicks off in East London next May, would move on to Sin City after its run in the UK.

And if they sign the deal, the band — made up of Agnetha Fältskog, Björn Ulvaeus, Benny Andersson and Anni-Frid Lyngstad – will be laughing all the way to the bank.

A source said: “There is a huge appetite for Abba’s new show Voyage in Las Vegas.

"MGM resorts, Caesars Palace and Resorts World are desperate to secure it and have been locked in a bidding war over it.

“Abba’s appeal is global and it would be a huge showcase to have this new avatar technology on the Vegas Strip.

“Talks are are in the early stages between promoters and the big three casinos here for ­entertainment.

“The word is that the major decision, aside from the money deal, is about the best fit for the sound facility and creating an intimate crowd.

“The dream would be for the show to move to the States after its London run and in Vegas it’s believed it could make almost £1billion.

“Plans at the moment are ­centred around a suggested 2024 opening which would coincide with the 50th anniversary of their Eurovision win and the release of chart-topper Waterloo.”

Abba’s Voyage show begins at the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park on May 27.

It will see the group appearing as digitally created versions of themselves alongside a ten-piece band.

Speaking about the show and its meaning, Bjorn said recently: “I have been thinking about that a lot, about what it actually means existentially.

"In that respect I am eternal. And we were the first ones to do that.

“And the first ones are always remembered and always referred to in the future — they were the pioneers.

“For me, I look at myself as an avatar from 1979 and I don’t even think it’s weird.”

Long-time no speak

ROD Stewart says he can only ­communicate with long-time “frenemy” Elton John through the media after the ­Rocketman star stopped returning his calls.

The legendary singers have traded countless barbs over the years but Rod says his attempt at ­calling a truce fell on deaf ears.

He said: “He is my old mate. We don’t speak to each other much any more. I miss him.”

Asked why he doesn’t just pick up the phone, Rod continued: “I did. I invited his sons to play on my football pitch and never got a reply.

"We have not spoken since. He just had a hip op so I wish him well. We still love each other.”

Speaking last November, Elton claimed he was keen to end the feud once and for all, saying: “There’s no point carrying on vendettas.”

I have a feeling these two will never truly bury the hatchet.

Gary fears a Covid KO

TERRIFIED Gary Barlow has been forced to put extra security measures in place to stop fans coming too close during his UK tour.

The Take That singer kicked off his Christmas trek in Belfast over the weekend but admits he thinks it will be a “miracle” if he makes it to the end of the dates due to the ongoing pandemic.

Gary is so scared of the new Covid variant that he has even stopped watching the news.

He said: “We just want to make it through to the end of the tour, which will be a miracle. I won’t be coming near anybody. If fans start trying to come near us we are going to be running.”

He added: “We have to be so strict. One bad encounter and that could be the tour gone.

“I am really going to keep myself to myself. I am going to be slipping in and out of the venues and hotels quickly.

“I want to make sure I am on stage every night.”

The singer, who is on the road with former X Factor winner Leona Lewis, added: “I’m trying not to watch the news as it scares the life out of me but right now the show is great.”
