I was sent a penis in the mail as a nasty prank but I got revenge

 Posted on July 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  410 words  |  Martina Birk

A CALIFORNIA woman who was sent a chocolate penis in the mail as a nasty prank is getting revenge.

Linda Parks was sent a life-sized chocolate penis from a company called D**k at Your Door and claims it caused her emotional distress.

D**k at Your Door is a company that sends phallic chocolates and other gag gifts anonymously.

Parks, a Ventura County Supervisor, received the unexpected delivery in June 2021.

At the time, an effort was underway to recall her position after she voted to sue businesses that failed to comply with Covid regulations.

However, that effort failed and Parks maintained her position.

Along with the chocolate penis was a note that allegedly read “Enjoy your early retirement... You deserve it," according to VCReporter.

Parks ultimately got revenge – by filing a lawsuit against D**k at Your Door.

The legal complaint alleges that the company failed and refused "to disclose the person(s) ultimately responsible for the offensive and outrageous conduct alleged herein."

Parks alleges that the actions of the company were "outrageous, intentional, and malicious, and done with reckless disregard" for the emotional and physical distress she would be caused.

In addition, the "chocolate d**k" is described as having "no redeeming social qualities, whatsoever."

She is suing for general damages, special damages including psychological counseling and lost wages, and also the cost of the suit.

The D**k at Your Door website says they ship all gifts "with complete discretion."

Under the FAQ page, the question "Why?" is met with the following response from the company: "Because what is better than sending an anonymous chocolate d**k to someone in the mail? There is something really special about knowing you have given something confusing with no hope of figuring out who the culprit is.

"That’s a special kind of torture."

The website also has a disclaimer stating that the products are intended as gag gifts and should not be sent with malicious intent.

This is not the first time a suit has emerged against D**k at Your Door.

In 2016, Vice News reports an employee at a Dallas-based technology company also sued D**k at Your Door.
